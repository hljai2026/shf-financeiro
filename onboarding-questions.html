Session Guide · Onboarding

Initial Questions for Cleyton

21 questions across 5 blocks — designed to understand the business as it actually operates

SessionMay 30, 2026 · Session 2
Estimated time40–50 minutes
Blocks required today1, 2, and 3
No assumptions about the spreadsheet Listen first, diagnose after
How to use this guide: Ask each question exactly as written and let Cleyton answer fully before moving on. Do not open the spreadsheet while he's explaining — let him describe his system in his own words. Note the terms he uses (he may call things by different names). If an answer is vague, follow up with: "Can you give me a concrete example — what happened last week?"
1
Required today · ~10 min
The Spreadsheet — Explain It to Me
5 questions
›
Q1
"Was this spreadsheet created by you, or did you inherit it from Chris when you bought the store?"
If inherited: does Chris use the same spreadsheet in his other stores?
If you created it: how long have you been using it?
Q2
"When you walk into the store and want to see how the week is going — what do you open first?"
Goal: find out which tab is actually used daily in practice.
Q3
"Tell me what tabs this spreadsheet has and what each one means to you day-to-day."
Let him list and explain in his own words. Don't name the tabs yourself.
Q4
"Is there any number you try to find in the spreadsheet but can't? Anything you want to see that isn't easy to get to?"
Q5
"Is there any part of the spreadsheet you stopped using — or that always has errors and you've given up on?"
2
Required today · ~10 min
The Money — From Start to Finish
4 questions
›
Q6
"Walk me through a cash sale from start to finish — from the moment the customer pays to when the money is in your account."
Does the money go directly to your account, or does it pass through Chris first?
Is any portion of it sent to Chris's account?
Q7
"What about a financed sale — Synchrony, Kafene, or any other — what happens? Does the payout go to your account or Chris's first?"
How long does it usually take to arrive after the sale?
Can you track what each financing company owes you at any given time?
Q8
"Do you have a separate bank account for the store, or is everything mixed in with your personal account?"
If separate: which bank? One account or more than one?
Q9
"At the end of the month, how do you know how much the store brought in and how much is left over?"
Goal: understand whether he has a formal close, a mental estimate, or no visibility at all.
3
Required today · ~15 min
The Settlement with Chris
5 questions
›
Q10
"Explain to me how the financial settlement between you and Chris works — what's the structure?"
How often do you settle up — daily, weekly, monthly?
Who initiates it — you, him, or is it automatic?
Q11
"When you owe something to Chris — inventory purchases, franchise fees, etc. — how is that tracked? Is there a document, an email, a WhatsApp message, or is it in memory?"
Q12
"And when Chris owes you something — financing payouts, commissions, transfers — how do you track what he still needs to pay you?"
Q13
"Has there ever been a disagreement between you two about a number — you thought he owed you more, or he thought you owed him more? How did you resolve it?"
Q14
"Was this settlement arrangement put in writing anywhere — a contract, an email, a WhatsApp message?"
4
If time allows · ~10 min
Inventory & Vendors
4 questions
›
Q15
"When you buy inventory — furniture, beds, mattresses — do you buy directly from the vendor or does it go through Chris?"
If through Chris: does he charge a markup, or is it at cost?
If direct: which vendors do you already have open accounts with?
Q16
"Do you know right now, roughly, what inventory you have on the floor — how many items, what it cost, what it's worth?"
Is that tracked anywhere?
Q17
"Is all the inventory in the store yours — you've fully paid for it — or is some of it still owed to Chris or a vendor?"
Q18
"When a customer places a custom order, how does that work — do you order from the vendor before or after they pay you?"
5
If time allows · ~5 min
What You Most Want to Fix
3 questions
›
Q19
"If you could fix one financial thing about the store today — just one — what would it be?"
Q20
"Is there any number that keeps you up at night — something you're worried about but can't see clearly?"
Q21
"When you picture the financials organized 6 months from now — what changes in your day-to-day?"
Notes for conducting the interview
›Don't open the spreadsheet while he's talking — let him describe his world first
›Write down every term he uses — he may call things by names that differ from what's in the sheet
›If the answer is vague: "Give me a concrete example — what happened last week?"
›Blocks 1, 2, and 3 are required today. Blocks 4 and 5 if time allows.
›Estimated time for all 5 blocks: 40–50 minutes
After this block — what comes next
1Compare his answers to what was seen in the spreadsheet — identify gaps and contradictions
2Update the Risk Register with confirmed vs. resolved items
3Walk through the Settlement Ledger v1 line by line to get confirmed totals for Batches 3–6
